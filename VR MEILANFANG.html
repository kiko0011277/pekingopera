Digital Mei Lanfang




Project introduction

The Digital Mei Lanfang Project, developed by the virtual avatar lab of the Beijing Institute of Technology (BIT), aims to recreate the 26-year-old style of famous Peking Opera artist Mei Lanfang through modern digital technology. The project, jointly launched by the Beijing Institute of Technology and the Central Academy of Drama (CAD) in 2020, aims to create an interactive "digital Peking Opera artist".

The Meticulous Process



The project team applied information technology and artificial intelligence to digitize and visualize everything from Mei Lanfang's face, posture and voice to her costumes and props. This included collecting a large number of historical photos and working with professionals from the Central Academy of Fine Arts to make head sculptures based on the photos, which were then scanned with high-precision laser scanners to obtain Mei Lanfang's digital facial structure.

In addition, in order to improve accuracy, the project team also invited plastic surgery experts to provide advice, and found a Mei Lanfang impersonator, collected her facial data and basic expressions, and then "transplanted" into the digital image.

In the future, the team plans to further promote the art of Peking Opera by enabling viewers to enjoy and interact virtually in real time through VR headsets. The project not only provides an innovative way for the dissemination and promotion of Peking Opera art, but also sets a model for the modern inheritance of traditional culture.

Accompanied by the melodious music of Beijing and Hu, three spotlights were lit in sequence. 26-year-old Mei Lanfang held a folding fan and walked to the center of the stage with light steps...
