The Digital process of Peking Opera In CHINA



Since the beginning of The 2010s, the digital process of Beijing Opera has not stopped, including not only the innovation in the form of communication, but also the combination of Beijing Opera and digital technology.

This chapter will analyze the digital road of Chinese Peking Opera from three parts: the digital equipment of Chinese Peking Opera theater, the digital content of Peking Opera, and the feedback of Peking Opera audience.




The digital development of Peking Opera theaters



There are now 200 Peking Opera theaters throughout China. This project selected a sample of 50 ones, surveyed their digital device penetration rates, and analyzed the data.

Analysis of the Digital Process of Peking Opera



The policy determines the digital development of Peking Opera

After the promulgation of new policies in the field of culture in 2012 and 2018, the digital equipment of many theaters showed rapid growth

The policy determines the digital development of Peking Opera

The policy has also promoted the digitization of Peking Opera resources, for example, the peak of the digitization process of Peking Opera scripts is the same as the peak of the growth of theater equipment

The policy determines the digital development of Peking Opera

The same has happened with the digitization of Peking Opera records, with both databases developing rapidly after the promulgation of cultural policies in 2012 and 2018

The external environment influences the digital development direction

In the wake of the COVID-19 outbreak, the external environment has promoted the digitalization of Peking Opera, forcing the industry to pay more attention to online communication



The digitization process of Beijing Opera is closely related to the policy support. Since the early 2010s, the Chinese government has begun to promote the protection and development of traditional culture and art such as Peking Opera, and the emergence of the digital peak of Peking Opera is often accompanied by the introduction of major policies. For example, in the Outline of the National Cultural Reform and Development Plan (12th Five-Year Plan) released in 2012, the goal of strengthening the protection and development of traditional opera art was clearly put forward, and during this period, the speed of the digitalization of Peking Opera was significantly accelerated. The introduction of these support policies has greatly promoted the digital development of Peking Opera, which is not only reflected in the update of the digital equipment of the offline theater, but also reflected in the improvement of the Peking Opera database on the Internet. In addition, local governments have also introduced subsidy policies for the digitalization of Peking Opera, such as online ticket purchase subsidy policies launched by the governments of Beijing, Shanghai, Jiangsu, Guangzhou and Shenzhen. The support of these policies has provided a solid foundation and impetus for the digitalization of Peking Opera.
